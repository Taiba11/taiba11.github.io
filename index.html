I'm an AI researcher working on multimodal and human-centered AI, with a focus on the sounds machines make to deceive us, and the emotions humans encode in every word. I recently completed my PhD at Sapienza University of Rome (ALCOR Lab), where my thesis tackled one of the defining problems of the synthetic media era: robust audio deepfake detection.

Along the way, I spent a research secondment at Fraunhofer IDMT in Germany under the EU's AI4Media project, building interpretable hybrid models that fuse phonetic features with emotion embeddings. Before that, my master's research at IIUM Malaysia pioneered speech emotion recognition architectures that earned a best paper award at IEEE ICSIMA.

My work spans capsule networks, diffusion transformers, self-supervised and continual learning, published at venues like CVPR workshops, ACM Multimedia, ICPR and IEEE journals. Now I'm a Postdoctoral Fellow at Istanbul Technical University, working on Meetween, a personal AI mediator for virtual meetings.

featured frameworks

Three ways to catch a fake voice

Synthetic speech keeps evolving. So do my detectors: each framework hears the forgery from a different angle.

ABC-CapsNet

CVPR Workshops 2024
problem → deepfake cues hide in part-whole structure

An Attention-Based Cascaded Capsule Network that preserves the spatial hierarchies CNNs throw away. Capsules model how spectral parts compose a voice, and attention routes the evidence, exposing forgeries that fool flat architectures.

ASTDT

EURASIP Journal on Information Security 2025
problem → detectors are black boxes nobody can audit

An interpretable Adaptive Spectro-Temporal Diffusion Transformer. It learns where forgeries live across time and frequency, and shows its reasoning, so a verdict of "fake" comes with evidence you can actually inspect.

Multi-Scale Self-Supervised Learning

IEEE Signal Processing Letters 2026
problem → labeled deepfake data can't keep up with generators

An efficient detector that learns from unlabeled audio at multiple temporal scales, capturing both micro-artifacts and long-range inconsistencies, and generalizing to synthesis methods it has never been shown.
